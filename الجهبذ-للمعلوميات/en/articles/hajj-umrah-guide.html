The Story of Hajj & Umrah Booking Platforms: From Landline to AI

Picture this: It's 1995, and you want to perform Umrah. What did you do? You went to a travel agency downtown, waited in line for hours, filled out a paper form, paid in cash, and waited — maybe two weeks — to find out if your booking went through. The hotel? You had no idea what it looked like beyond a glossy brochure with an overly edited photo. Transportation? The agency's car, and they set the schedule. You were just a passenger on the trip, not a decision-maker.

Now fast forward to 2026. What's the difference? Night and day. Today you can open an app on your phone — while at home, in your pajamas — and compare prices of 50 hotels in Makkah in 3 minutes. Read reviews from people who booked before you. See real photos, not stock images. Book and pay online. Print your visa (or rather — get it as a QR code). Choose your Umrah company from a ministry-licensed list. Compare offers from 10 companies on a single screen. And be done within a quarter of an hour.

The story isn't just that technology has advanced. The story is that the Hajj and Umrah market has completely transformed — from a closed market limited to specific agencies and traditional travel offices, to an open, transparent market serving millions of Muslims each year. In 2025, Saudi Arabia received over 15 million Umrah performers and 1.8 million Hajj pilgrims. An enormous number. And behind every pilgrim and Umrah performer is a chain of digital services helping organize the journey.

Historic Moment: In 2019, the Saudi Ministry of Hajj and Umrah launched the "Mosafer" platform — the first integrated digital platform for organizing Umrah trips. Then came the "Nusuk" platform, bringing all services together in one place. By 2025, over 80% of Umrah visas were issued entirely electronically — without ever visiting an embassy.

This digital transformation hasn't just made booking easier — it has also lowered prices. Why? Because transparency creates competition. Every Umrah company now knows its ratings on the "Nusuk" platform are visible to everyone. And that any Umrah performer can compare its price against 10 other companies in 5 minutes. The result: higher quality and more realistic prices.

But not everything is rosy. With digital openness, new problems emerged: scams and fraud, fake companies, hotels with unrealistic photos, and contracts with hidden clauses. That's why we're here — to show you the real deal.

Types of Services: All-Inclusive, Hotels Only, Transportation — Choose What Fits

Want to perform Umrah today? You have 3 main scenarios:

1. All-Inclusive Package

The traditional, safest method for first-time travelers. Includes visa, flight tickets, accommodation, internal transportation (Makkah — Madinah — airport), and religious guidance in most cases.

  • Pros: Nothing to worry about. They handle everything. If your flight is delayed, they arrange the alternative.
  • Cons: More expensive. Limited options — you choose from their inventory, not the whole market. And often the hotel isn't the quality they promised.
  • Approximate price (2026): From $800 to $2,500+ — depending on the hotel, services, and peak season.

2. Hotel-Only Booking

The method that has become easiest thanks to platforms like Booking.com and Agoda. You choose your hotel yourself, read reviews, compare prices, and book in minutes.

  • Pros: Full control. You can pick exactly the hotel you want. Compare thousands of rooms on one screen. Also, Booking and Agoda prices are sometimes lower than package prices — especially if you book early.
  • Cons: You need to handle the remaining arrangements yourself (visa, flights, transportation). Not ideal for first-time travelers.

3. Transportation + Visa Only

The choice of adventurous and experienced travelers. Some companies provide only the visa and transportation from the airport to Makkah and between Makkah and Madinah. You choose your own hotels and flights.

  • Pros: Cheapest option. Full control over every detail of your trip.
  • Cons: You need to know what you're doing. If you get lost or something goes wrong, no one will help you.
Al-Jehabiz Tip: If this is your first Umrah, start with an all-inclusive package from a licensed company. Once you get familiar with the environment, try booking yourself next year — hotels on Booking and flights on Skyscanner. You'll save 30-40%.

Best Licensed Umrah Companies — Under the Saudi Ministry of Hajj

This is the most important point in the entire article: Licensing. There is a lot of fraud in the Umrah sector. Companies collect people's money and disappear. Companies promise 5-star hotels and you find yourself in a room without windows. How do you make sure a company is licensed?

The Saudi Ministry of Hajj and Umrah issues licenses to companies providing Umrah services — locally and internationally. Licensed companies are listed on the "Nusuk" (Nusuk) platform — the official authorized platform. If the company is not on Nusuk, do not deal with it. Plain and simple.

Flight Booking: Skyscanner and Smart Search

The second biggest expense in Umrah after accommodation — flight tickets. Especially during peak seasons: Ramadan, Dhul Qadah, and Hajj season. This is where Skyscanner comes in.

Skyscanner doesn't book directly — it's a search engine that compares ticket prices from hundreds of airlines and booking sites on one screen. Its advantages:

  • Whole month search: Instead of searching day by day, tell it "show prices for all days of Ramadan" — it'll show you a full calendar with the cheapest days.
  • Price alerts: Set a target price, and if the ticket drops below it, you get a notification.
  • Advanced filtering: Direct flights, one stop, flight duration, preferred airlines.
Quick Tip: In 2026, the cheapest tickets from Cairo to Jeddah during Umrah season range between $200 and $350 (round trip) if you book 2 months before travel. If you book a week before — the price can reach $600+. Book early = save 50%.

Travel Tips — What No One Tells You

These are practical tips gathered from traveler experiences our Al-Jehabiz team has interacted with. You won't find them in brochures:

  • Book Early = Big Savings: Hotel and flight prices during Umrah season (especially Ramadan) increase by 40-60% two weeks before the season. Book at least 3-4 months in advance.
  • Mandatory Travel Insurance: The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia requires health travel insurance as a condition for visa entry since 2025. Most Umrah companies include it in the package, but if you're self-booking — buy travel insurance that covers health care in Saudi Arabia.
  • Local SIM Card: Instead of paying for international roaming (roaming in Saudi Arabia is expensive), buy an STC or Mobily SIM at the airport. $20 gives you 20GB valid for a month — great for maps and WhatsApp.
  • Currency: While credit cards are accepted at all major hotels and malls, small vendors and local restaurants prefer cash. Exchange to Saudi Riyal in Egypt or your home country, and keep extra cash with you.
  • Essential Apps: Download "Tawakkalna" (for electronic registration on arrival), "Nusuk" (for booking appointments and services), and "Haramain Maps" (for navigation inside the Haram — shows entrances and exits).
  • Peak Times at the Haram: Friday prayers, Ramadan nights, and Taraweeh — the crowds are unbearable. If you can, pray at non-peak times during the week. If you must go — take water and a small prayer mat.
  • Distant Hotels Aren't Bad: In Makkah, hotels 10-15 minutes away (like Al-Aziziyah and Al-Zaher areas) are 50-60% cheaper than hotels adjacent to the Haram. And there are free shuttle buses every 10 minutes.
Nusuk App — Not Optional: Since 2025, the "Nusuk" (nusuk.sa) platform has become mandatory for every Umrah performer to register Umrah performance times and organize entry to the Haram. You must download the app and register before your arrival. Not only that — you also use it to book prayer times at Al-Rawda Al-Sharifa (Madinah) and prayers at the Grand Mosque during peak seasons.

Pros & Cons: DIY Booking vs All-Inclusive Package

In the end, this comparison most determines your decision. Do you invest your time and effort in booking yourself (and save money) or pay extra for peace of mind?

DIY Booking

  • Save 30-40% on all-inclusive package cost
  • Full control — choose your hotel, flights, and timing exactly
  • Complete flexibility — change or cancel anything yourself
  • Reviews & opinions — read from real people before booking
  • Loyalty points — earn from Booking, Agoda, Skyscanner

All-Inclusive Package

  • Peace of mind — no need to think about any detail
  • Full support — if something goes wrong, someone helps you
  • Great for beginners — first time in Umrah? The package is easier
  • Organized religious program — some companies provide religious guides and written prayers
  • Guaranteed visa — the company handles all visa procedures
