The Story of Agoda: From a Singaporean Startup to a Global Booking Giant

Agoda's story is proof that you don't have to be American or European to dominate — Agoda is Singaporean, 100%. Founded in 2005 by Michael Kenny (Irish) and Adrian Currie (British ex-pat). The idea: while Booking and Expedia were focused on Europe and America — they completely neglected Southeast Asia. The region lacked dedicated booking platforms that understood local payment methods and provided competitive prices. Agoda filled that gap perfectly.

The beginning was modest. The founders bootstrapped the company and competed fiercely against Booking.com and Priceline in Asia for years. But Agoda had a secret weapon: local market understanding. While international platforms struggled with Asian payment methods, Agoda offered Alipay, WeChat Pay, and local bank transfers in each country — from Thailand to Japan to Indonesia.

The turning point was 2007. Booking Holdings (then Priceline) acquired Agoda for an estimated $150-200 million. The logic was strategic: Booking Holdings wanted deep entry into the Asian market — and Agoda was the perfect Singaporean key. Today, Agoda operates as a nearly independent subsidiary in Singapore — with a massive team of over 3,000 employees, maintaining its unique identity separate from Booking.com.

Turning Point: In 2020 — during COVID — Agoda faced the biggest crisis in its history. The company laid off 25% of its workforce. But by 2023 it had fully recovered and returned to growth. In 2026, Agoda is the #1 hotel booking platform in 6 Asian countries and #2 in 8 others.

How Agoda Works — The Smartest Booking System

Agoda looks like Booking.com on the surface — but the internal mechanisms are different:

  • "Member Prices": Free registration (30 seconds) gives you access to special prices not visible otherwise. Always sign up.
  • Secret Deals: Like Hotwire but integrated into Agoda. You choose the area and star rating — you get 40% off the hotel.
  • AgodaCash: The loyalty currency — explained in detail below.
  • Smart filters: Very precise filters — "Free cancellation," "Breakfast included," "Pay at hotel," "Water park," "5-star luxury".

AgodaRewards — The Most Generous Loyalty Program

AgodaRewards is a points program: you earn AgodaCash with every booking, redeemable on future bookings. 1 point AgodaCash = $1 USD — usable on any hotel on the platform. Comparison with Genius on Booking.com: Genius gives you instant discounts. AgodaCash gives you spendable credit. Which is better? 10 Tips to Get the Best Price on Agoda

  1. Create an Agoda account: Member prices are 10-15% cheaper than what non-members see.
  2. Look for Secret Deals: Agoda offers "Secret Deals" in Asian cities — sometimes up to 40% off the public price.
  3. Use AgodaCash: Collect AgodaCash from every booking and use it to reduce future bookings.
  4. Compare across currencies: Member prices are usually cheapest — check both local and your own currency.
  5. Check "Deals" tab: Agoda always has seasonal deals — especially during Asian holidays.
  6. Book early: For Asia peak season (Chinese New Year, Songkran), booking 3 months ahead saves up to 30%.
  7. Use the app: The mobile app often offers additional discounts — exclusive app deals.
  8. Compare with Booking.com: For hotels outside Asia — compare both. The price can vary significantly.
  9. Check mystery deals: Agoda has a "mystery hotel" feature similar to Hotwire — savings up to 50% on 4-star hotels.
  10. Don't fall for fake discounts: Some hotels raise the base price then "discount" it — always compare the final price.
