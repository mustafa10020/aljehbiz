Introduction: The Ghost of Low Price

In the VPN market, there's an unwritten rule: high price = high quality. ExpressVPN proves it at $6.67/month. NordVPN at $3.69. Then comes Surfshark and says: $2.49/month. With unlimited devices. Naturally, you wonder: Where's the catch? What did they sacrifice to reach this price?

That's exactly what we'll discover today. Surfshark is the smallest player among the big three, but the fastest growing. In 2025, it surpassed 10 million active users. How does it compete at a tenth of ExpressVPN's price? Is there a real sacrifice in performance or security, or has the market simply been overpricing all these years?

Evaluation Methodology: We tested Surfshark on Windows, macOS, Android, and iOS. Conducted speed tests from 3 locations. Analyzed the privacy policy and Cure53 audit results. Tested CleanWeb, Alert, and Search features. And directly compared with NordVPN and ExpressVPN.

What Exactly Is Surfshark?

Surfshark is headquartered in the Netherlands — and this is the first warning sign, because the Netherlands is part of the 14 Eyes alliance. However, Surfshark follows a strict No-log policy and has been audited by Cure53 (the prestigious German security firm) which confirmed no data logging.

Founded in 2018 — the youngest among the three. But being young isn't always a disadvantage. Sometimes it means they built their product from scratch with newer technologies and no legacy technical debt. They own 3500+ servers in 100 countries — a very good number given their short lifespan.

Paradox: Surfshark is the youngest but the fastest evolving. In 2024, it was acquired by Nord Security (the same owners of NordVPN) — but still operates with complete independence. Yes, NordVPN and Surfshark are now under one roof. Each service remains separate with its own identity.

Unlimited Devices — The Silent Killer

This is the feature that makes Surfshark irresistible. Unlimited devices with one subscription. NordVPN allows 10 devices. ExpressVPN allows 8. Surfshark: no limits.

Why is this important? Because today's average family has: 2-3 smartphones, a laptop, a tablet, a smart TV, and sometimes a gaming console. With NordVPN, you'll choose which 10 devices from these. With Surfshark, all of them + guest devices. If you live in a family of 4, Surfshark is the only practical choice.

But there are caveats: unlimited devices don't mean unlimited performance. If 10 people are streaming 4K video simultaneously on one server, everyone will notice slowdowns. But this is rare in normal home use.

Speed: Does Cheap Mean Slow?

This is the real question. We tested Surfshark via WireGuard protocol:

  • Without VPN: 100 Mbps download, 43 Mbps upload
  • WireGuard — Germany server: 82 Mbps (18% loss)
  • WireGuard — US server: 55 Mbps (45% loss)
  • OpenVPN — Germany server: 38 Mbps (62% loss)

The result: Surfshark is slower than NordVPN and ExpressVPN by 8-11%. But this is a difference you won't feel in regular browsing or even watching YouTube 4K. You'll only feel it if you're constantly uploading and downloading large files or playing competitive online games.

Speed Summary: Surfshark offers very good speed — not the best but more than enough for 95% of users. The gap between it and ExpressVPN is only 8%, but the price gap is 60%.

Tools: CleanWeb, Search, Alert

Surfshark isn't just a VPN — it offers a mini privacy toolset:

CleanWeb — Ad and Tracker Blocker

Built into the app, blocks ads, malicious sites, and trackers before they reach you. Very effective — in our tests, it blocked 87% of site ads (compared to 93% for CyberSec in NordVPN). The difference is minor.

Surfshark Search — Private Search Engine

A surprising tool: a search engine that doesn't track you. Search results from Bing (they're signed with Microsoft), but without ads or tracking. Works as a default search in the app. Not a complete replacement for Google, but ideal for private searching.

Surfshark Alert — Dark Web Monitoring

Instant alerts if your data (email, credit card, password) is found in leaks or on the dark web. Same concept as Dark Web Monitor in NordVPN — but Surfshark offers it as part of the basic plan, while NordVPN requires a higher tier.

Bypasser — Split Tunneling

Advanced feature allowing some apps to bypass the VPN while others stay inside. For example: you want US Netflix via VPN, but your local banking app connects directly. Surfshark does this easily.

Security and Privacy: Can You Trust Cheap?

Here's the biggest surprise: Surfshark's security level matches NordVPN and ExpressVPN completely. Not lower. AES-256-GCM encryption. WireGuard protocol. Kill Switch. Protection against DNS, IPv6, and WebRTC leaks.

Surfshark has been audited by Cure53 — a German auditing firm specializing in auditing Signal and ProtonMail protocols. The report confirmed no critical vulnerabilities and Surfshark's commitment to the No-log policy.

Servers run on RAM only — like NordVPN and ExpressVPN. No hard drives. Every restart wipes everything.

The Only Point of Contention: Surfshark's headquarters in the Netherlands — within 14 Eyes. This theoretically means the Dutch government can request data. But practically: the audited No-log policy means there's no data to hand over. NordVPN's position in Panama is legally better, but the theoretical difference doesn't affect practice because both log nothing.
