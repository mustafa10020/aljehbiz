Hotels.com 2026: Why Pay for 10 Nights and Get 1 Free?

Hotels.com isn't just a hotel booking site — it's one of the strongest players in the Expedia Group (with Expedia, Vrbo, and Orbitz). It has 500,000+ hotels in 200+ countries, and a rewards program that lets you collect 10 nights for one free. But is it better than Booking.com?

The Beginning — From a Simple Site to a Global Hotel Empire

In 1991, Expedia Group (then part of Microsoft) launched Hotels.com as a simple online hotel booking site. At a time when bookings were made by phone or by walking to the hotel yourself — Hotels.com made online booking easy and instant.

In 2001, Expedia (the company) acquired it — and today Hotels.com is part of the largest travel group in the world (Expedia Group) alongside Expedia, Vrbo, Orbitz, Travelocity, and Hotwire. Hotels.com has 500,000+ hotels in 200+ countries, supports 40+ languages and 90+ currencies — including AED, SAR, JOD, and EGP.

The difference between Hotels.com and Booking.com: Booking focuses on "any type of accommodation" (apartments, villas, floating houses), while Hotels.com focuses on traditional hotels with uniform service.

Rewards Program: Collect 10 nights — get 1 free. The average value of the free night = the average value of the 10 nights you booked. If you booked 10 nights at an average of $100 — you get a free night worth $100. Simple, direct, no complicated calculations.

Rewards Program — Hotels.com's Hidden Gem

The Hotels.com Rewards program (formerly known as Welcome Rewards) is the strongest reason to use Hotels.com instead of any other booking site. The idea is simple:

  • Every night you book through Hotels.com (any hotel, any price) — you collect a stamp
  • Every 10 stamps = 1 free night worth the average of the 10 nights you booked
  • The stamp is counted per night, not per booking — 10 bookings of 3 nights each = 30 stamps
  • The free night has no expiration date (typically) — you can use it anytime
  • You can use the free night at any hotel from 500,000+ — full coverage

Comparison with Booking Genius: Genius gives 10-20% off on every booking (must be a hotel enrolled in the program). Hotels.com gives a free night every 10 nights (10% return) — but all hotels are included. Booking Genius is better if you travel frequently to specific hotels. Hotels.com Rewards is better if you travel to a variety of hotels.

Features That Set It Apart from Booking and Expedia

  • Direct Rewards Program: No points, no tiers, no calculations. 10 nights = 1 free night. Booking has Genius tiers (3 levels), Expedia has Expedia Rewards with points. Hotels.com is the simplest
  • Free cancellation: Most hotels offer free cancellation up to 24-48 hours before arrival. Booking same. Expedia has some non-refundable bookings
  • Price Match: If you find the same hotel cheaper on another site — Hotels.com matches the price and gives you 100% of the difference as a coupon
  • Excellent mobile app: Rated 4.7 stars — better than Expedia (4.5) and less than Booking (4.8)
  • Real reviews: Hotels.com uses a 1-10 rating system (more than stars) — gives more accuracy. Booking also uses 1-10. Expedia uses 1-5
  • Arabic support: Full Arabic interface, Arabic customer support, and Arabic currencies (SAR, AED, EGP, JOD)

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Rewards program — simplest and most transparent (10 nights = 1 free night)
  • 500,000+ hotels — excellent coverage of major cities
  • Price Match — guarantees cheapest price with 100% difference back
  • Free cancellation — most bookings cancellable up to 24 hours
  • Excellent app — 4.7 stars on App Store and Play Store
  • Full Arabic support — interface, currencies, customer support

Cons

  • Hotels only — no apartments or villas like Booking
  • No flight + hotel packages (unlike Expedia)
  • Some prices are higher than Booking for the same hotels — must compare
  • Rewards take time — 10 nights = 1 free night, if you travel once a year it's hard to accumulate
  • Same booking site problem: prices vary by location and device
  • Some hotels on the site are inactive or not updated
