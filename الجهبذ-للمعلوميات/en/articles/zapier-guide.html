The Beginning — The Story of the Man Who Got Tired of Moving Data

In 2012, Wade Foster needed to move data between Gmail, Twitter, and Evernote. Each tool required a different API, different code, different time. He got tired. He sat down with Bryan Helmig and Mike Knoop — and they decided to build something simple: connect tools without code. Thus Zapier was born.

The idea is simple: "If This, Then That" — but for professionals. Not just notifications — data transfer: when a new email arrives in Gmail, create a task in Trello. When a column updates in Google Sheets, send a Slack message.

In 2014, they joined Y Combinator. In 2018 — 1 million users. 2024 — 2.2 million companies (including 80% of Fortune 500). Zapier isn't just a tool — it's the layer that connects all your tools. Without it, each tool is an island. With it, the islands connect with bridges.

In 2020, Zapier acquired Makerpad (a no-code education academy). The message: Zapier isn't just a tool — it's a movement. A movement teaching people they can do things without a programmer. Today, 100+ million automations run daily on Zapier.

What Is Zapier

Zapier is an automation platform (iPaaS — Integration Platform as a Service) that connects 7,000+ different apps. The basic idea: Zap — a link between two or more apps. The Zap has two parts:

  • Trigger: The event that starts the chain (e.g., "When a new email arrives in Gmail")
  • Action: The event that happens next (e.g., "Create a card in Trello")

You can add multiple steps: Trigger → Action 1 → Action 2 → Action 3 — each step depends on the one before. You can also add conditions (Filters) — "If the email is from an important client, send a notification. If spam, ignore."

Zaps, Multi-Step & Filters — From Simple to Complex

Zapier's power isn't in simple connections — it's in controlled complexity. You can build complex workflows with:

  • Multi-Step Zaps: 3, 5, 10, even 50 steps in one Zap. For example: when a Typeform submission comes in → create a row in Google Sheets → send a confirmation message in Slack → add the user to Mailchimp list → create a follow-up task in Asana → send a welcome email
  • Filters: Smart conditions — "Only execute this step if value X = Y". Filtering reduces noise and increases accuracy
  • Paths: Branching paths — if condition A, execute path A. If condition B, execute path B (not available in all plans)
  • Formatter: Data transformation — numbers, dates, text — before moving between apps
  • Schedule: Scheduled Zaps — run the Zap every day at 9 AM
  • Delay: Delay — wait 60 minutes before executing the next step

The more steps you add, the more Tasks are consumed — and the higher the cost. Tip: design simple Zaps (3-5 steps) and use Filters from the start to save.

Zapier AI — Smart Automation in 2026

In 2024-2026, Zapier entered the AI world with force. New features:

  • Zapier AI (Chat Interface): Instead of building the Zap yourself, you can write a sentence: "When an email arrives in Gmail with subject 'Invoice', create a task in Trello and log it in Google Sheets and send a Slack message" — and Zapier AI builds the Zap automatically
  • AI Actions: You can use AI as a step in the Zap — for example: "When a customer writes a review, use AI to analyze their sentiment, and if negative, send a notification to the manager"
  • Natural Language Filters: Instead of code filters, you can write "if the email is about a customer complaint" — and AI understands and classifies

Not to mention GPT Actions: you can connect the Zap to ChatGPT API and use it as a data processor before moving to another tool. Weakness: plans with AI are more expensive ($69/month per person).
