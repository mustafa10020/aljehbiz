What Exactly Is ExpressVPN?

ExpressVPN is a company headquartered in the British Virgin Islands — outside the 14 Eyes alliance, and not subject to data retention laws. Founded in 2009 and sold to Kape Technologies in 2021. Before you jump out the window — Kape pledged ExpressVPN's independence, and there's no evidence of its impact on user privacy.

ExpressVPN owns over 3000 servers in 105 countries. Not the most numerous (NordVPN surpasses it), but all its servers run on RAM only — no hard drives — an architecture they pioneered.

Lightway: The Protocol Built From Scratch

Most VPN companies use ready-made protocols: OpenVPN (slow but secure) or WireGuard (fast but less mature). ExpressVPN decided to build its own protocol — Lightway.

Lightway is built on the WolfSSL library — a very lightweight encryption library. The result: smaller data packets, faster connections, lower battery consumption. Most importantly: the code is fully open source and any security expert can audit it.

Open Source — Guaranteed Trust: The Lightway protocol is available on GitHub. This ensures no backdoors. Very few VPN companies do this.

Comparison with NordVPN protocols:

  • Lightway vs NordLynx: NordLynx is 3-5% faster, but Lightway proved more stable on flaky networks.
  • Lightway vs OpenVPN: Lightway is 300-400% faster. No contest.
  • Lightway security: wolfSSL + AES-256 — same military-grade security with smaller packet size.

Speed Tests: Blazing Fast — But?

ExpressVPN claims blazing speed — and in our tests, it's true that ExpressVPN is very fast, especially over Lightway:

  • Without VPN (baseline): 100 Mbps download, 45 Mbps upload
  • Lightway — Germany server: 90 Mbps (10% loss)
  • Lightway — US server: 65 Mbps (35% loss)
  • OpenVPN — Germany server: 48 Mbps (52% loss)

The result: ExpressVPN is very fast — but NordLynx (NordVPN) is still faster by a 3-5% margin. The difference isn't significant for the average user, but it's there for those who want every bit of performance.

Speed Summary: ExpressVPN is among the fastest VPN services — NordVPN barely edges it out. The difference isn't noticeable in daily browsing, but shows in large file downloads and ultra-HD streaming.

Security and Encryption: TrustedServer

ExpressVPN was the first VPN company to offer the TrustedServer architecture — all servers run on RAM only. Upon restart, everything disappears. This means:

  • Nothing is logged: Even if a government seizes the server, there's no data.
  • Instant updates: Every server is rebooted from a clean image on each connection.
  • AES-256 encryption: Same standard as the US military. Unbreakable in the foreseeable future.

Kill Switch is built-in at the system level — if VPN drops, the internet stops completely. Network Lock (its name in ExpressVPN) works on any platform.

User Experience: Deceptive Simplicity

This is ExpressVPN's greatest strength — simplicity. The ExpressVPN app is the easiest VPN app we've used:

  • One button: connect / disconnect
  • Country selection: simple list or interactive map
  • Settings: limited but sufficient. No mental fatigue from too many options.
  • Installation: 3 minutes and done.

If you're a beginner or don't want to tinker with settings, ExpressVPN is the best fit. But if you like customization (choosing protocol, toggling encryption settings, extra tools) — NordVPN or Surfshark give you more freedom.

Pros and Cons

Cons
