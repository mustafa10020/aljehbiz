Is ClickUp Really "The Everything Alternative"?

In 2017, ClickUp founder Zeb Evans had a problem: his team used 7 different tools to manage their work — Trello for tasks, Google Docs for documents, Sheets for spreadsheets, Slack for communication, Zoom for meetings, Drive for files, and Asana for planning. Each tool had a piece of the picture.

So he decided to do something crazy: One tool to replace all of these tools. He named it ClickUp. Its slogan: "One app to replace them all."

After 9 years and updates almost every two weeks, ClickUp today is the most comprehensive project management tool on the market. Not just tasks and a calendar — no, it has: documents, Goals, automation, Chat, Whiteboard, time tracking, Gantt Charts, Mind Maps, Calendar, and over 1,000 ready-made templates.

But with great power comes great responsibility. Many new users complain about over-complexity. This article breaks down ClickUp piece by piece: what it does well, where it falls short, and who it's exactly for.

What Makes It Different from Notion & Asana?

The biggest difference between ClickUp and its competitors is vertical depth. While Notion focuses on horizontal flexibility (databases and linking everything), ClickUp focuses on the fine details of project management:

  • Each task: 30+ custom fields — text, number, date, dropdown, checkbox, label, currency, and even links to other tables
  • 5 levels of hierarchy: Space → Folder → List → Task → Subtask
  • Custom statuses — not just "To Do / In Progress / Done", you can create "Review / On Hold / Testing" etc.
  • Priorities: Urgent, High, Normal, Low — with auto-filtering and sorting

This depth is why product and engineering teams at medium and large companies choose ClickUp over Asana because they need precise control over every detail.

Features in Detail — What Can You Do in ClickUp?

Task Management — ClickUp's Beating Heart

Tasks in ClickUp aren't just "title + description." Each task is an integrated information container:

  • Custom Fields: 30+ field types, you can create a "Budget" field linked to a database, or a "Task Type" field with a custom dropdown
  • Subtasks: Nested sub-tasks with unlimited levels
  • Checklists: Checklists inside the task — very useful for QA and launch processes
  • Dependencies: "Task B can't start until Task A is done"
  • Time Tracking: Built-in time tracking — see how many hours each team member spent on a task
  • Comments with Assignees: Comment and @mention a member to request a reply
  • Proofing & Approvals: Review and approval — useful for marketing and design teams

Goals — Connecting Tasks to Strategy

One of ClickUp's strongest and rarest features. You can create quarterly or annual goals and link them to specific tasks. For example: "Increase sales by 20%" — link it to a "Marketing campaign" task and a "Product development" task. As the tasks progress, the goal automatically progresses. You see a live Dashboard with progress %.

Notion has something similar with databases and relations, but ClickUp does it in an easier and faster way for teams focused on OKRs and KPIs.

Docs

ClickUp has a built-in document editor, but it's not as powerful as Notion's Wiki or databases. ClickUp Docs are more suitable for documenting tasks and short procedures. You can link a doc to a specific project, add ready-made tasks, and share the link with the team.

Tip: Use ClickUp Docs for short operational documents (SOPs), and use Notion for long Wiki and complex databases. Each tool in its place.

15+ Ways to View Your Projects

Unlike Notion which gives you 6 views, ClickUp gives you over 15 different views:

  • List View: Traditional list — with Group By and sub-sections
  • Board View: Kanban board — drag and drop between columns
  • Box View: Custom cards — similar to Notion Gallery
  • Calendar View: Calendar — see tasks by date
  • Gantt View: Timeline — for long-term planning projects (construction, product launch)
  • Mind Map View: Mind map — brainstorming and organizing ideas
  • Timeline View: Timeline — display tasks in chronological sequence
  • Activity View: Activity log — track every change
  • Workload View: Workload — see how many tasks each employee has and when they reach capacity
  • Map View: Geographic map — for projects with locations
  • Table View: Table — similar to spreadsheets

One task database, 11 different ways to display it. Each view has its own filter, sort, and Group By.

Automations & Integrations — The Real Magic

ClickUp has built-in automation (no Zapier needed). You can create rules like:

  • If task status changes to "Done" → send a Slack notification
  • If task priority increases to Urgent → change Assignee to manager
  • If deadline approaches → add a reminder comment
  • If a task is assigned to someone → add a start date 3 days from now

For integrations: ClickUp integrates with 1,000+ tools — Slack, Google Drive, Zoom, GitLab, GitHub, Figma, Loom, HubSpot, Salesforce... the list is very long.

ClickUp vs Notion

Notion suits you if: you need Wiki + databases + tasks in one tool, with high flexibility. ClickUp suits you if: you need deep project management with Gantt Charts, Goals, Workload, Automations.

Asana is easier and faster to learn — suitable for small teams that want something that "works immediately" without setup. ClickUp requires longer setup, but provides more power after setup.

Pros & Cons

Pros:
  • The most comprehensive in the market — covers almost everything
  • Powerful built-in automation without Zapier
  • Workload View — visibility into each team member's workload
  • Goals — connect tasks to strategic objectives
  • Gantt & Timeline — for long-term planning projects
  • Weekly updates — very fast development
  • Strong API — build custom integrations
Cons:
  • Very steep learning curve — worse than Notion
  • Cluttered interface — too many options confuse new users
  • Free plan limited in advanced features
  • Performance slows with large projects (1000+ tasks)
  • Wiki system not as strong as Notion
  • Mobile app slower than competitors

Verdict — Is ClickUp Right for You?

Great for: Product teams, engineering teams, medium companies that need automation, reports, and deep task management
Not ideal for: One person or a very small team wanting simplicity — Asana is better
Overall Rating: 8.5/10 — A powerful tool after setup, but the setup itself is a journey

If you manage a team of 5-50 people and need precise control over tasks, goals, and reports — ClickUp is the strongest choice. But if your priority is Wiki and absolute flexibility — Try Notion.
