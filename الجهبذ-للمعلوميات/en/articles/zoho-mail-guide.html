In the world of professional email, the two big names are Google Workspace and Microsoft 365. They're like Coca-Cola and Pepsi — dominating the market with their size and history. But in the other corner, there's a small company from India called Zoho, fighting stubbornly. It's not offering an "inferior" product — it's offering a different product: much cheaper, with respectable capabilities, and a free spirit.

In this guide, we'll put Zoho Mail under the microscope: the free plan (which is honestly generous), the unbelievable prices, real performance, security, and comparison with Google Workspace — The Complete Solution and Microsoft 365 — The Most Widespread. The Story: From India to the World — The Underdog Challenging Google and Microsoft

Sridhar Vembu — a name not as famous as Sundar Pichai or Satya Nadella. But this man founded Zoho in 1996 (yes, before Google!), in the outskirts of Chennai, India. The idea: "Why do tech companies have to be in Silicon Valley? We'll build from India at a lower price."

Zoho started as an enterprise tech tools company — CRM, support, accounting. In 2008, it entered the email arena with Zoho Mail. The timing was ironic — Google held 70% of the free email market, and Microsoft held 60% of the professional email market. Zoho entered as the "cheap alternative" — and that worked in its favor.

Startups in developing countries — India, Brazil, Egypt, Nigeria, Indonesia — embraced Zoho quickly. Suddenly, a startup with 5 employees could get professional email for free — with their own domain, no ads, no borders. Regular Gmail is free but with a gmail.com domain — not suitable for business. Zoho offers free business email.

Shocking Fact: Zoho today has over 80 million users across its products. The company is still fully owned by its founder — no venture capital funding. And its headquarters is in a small village called "Tenkasi" in South India — not in Bangalore or Mumbai. Sridhar Vembu rejected acquisition offers from Microsoft and Oracle — which explains why Zoho doesn't follow Google's privacy policies.

Zoho Mail Interface — Premium Experience or Tired Classic?

Zoho Mail's interface resembles Gmail — noticeably. The buttons are in roughly the same places, labels, search, and filters. The difference: Zoho is slightly slower to load than Gmail, but stronger in organization.

Unique Interface Features

  • Streams: Internal messaging system similar to Slack but within the same email interface. You can create a Stream for a specific project and all emails related to the project appear in one Stream. Excellent for small teams.
  • Mail Queue: If you send an email by mistake, you can retract it before the recipient opens it. Not available in Gmail or Outlook. The service works for over 30 seconds after sending.
  • Custom Domain Alias: You can add multiple domain names to one account for free. If you have company.com, company.net, and company.org — all can send and receive from the same mailbox.
  • Smart Tabs: Just like Gmail — Primary, Social, Promotions — but Zoho gives more control over distribution rules.
  • Full Arabic Support: The interface is available in Arabic, and technical support in Arabic exists — though not 24/7.
However: Zoho's interface isn't as fast as Gmail. Sometimes you feel a "heaviness" in browsing — especially if you have thousands of emails in one folder. This is because the interface uses more JavaScript than lightweight HTML. Google has 20 years of experience improving Gmail's performance — Zoho is still learning.

Zoho Workplace — Not Just Email, an Integrated Environment

Zoho is not just email — it has Workplace, the real competitor to Google Workspace and Microsoft 365. Workplace includes:

  • Zoho Mail — Email (what we're discussing)
  • Zoho Writer — Word processor (alternative to Google Docs / Word)
  • Zoho Sheet — Spreadsheets (alternative to Sheets / Excel)
  • Zoho Show — Presentations (alternative to Slides / PowerPoint)
  • Zoho Cliq — Messaging (alternative to Google Chat / Teams)
  • Zoho Meeting — Video conferencing (alternative to Meet / Zoom)
  • Zoho WorkDrive — Cloud storage (alternative to Drive / OneDrive)

I won't lie to you: Writer, Sheet, and Show are not as good as Google Docs and Sheets. Formatting is weaker, real-time collaboration is slower, the interface is less smooth. But for 90% of users — regular writing, simple spreadsheets, average presentations — the difference isn't noticeable. And it won't matter in your decision if you're saving 90% of email costs.

The plan that includes all of this is Zoho Workplace Standard — €4 monthly per user. Compared to $12 for Google Workspace Business Standard and $12.50 for Microsoft 365 Business Standard. Zoho is three times cheaper.

Security and Privacy — An Indian Company, Really?

A frequently asked question: "I trust Google or Microsoft, but an Indian company?" The idea is that Zoho is not an "ordinary Indian company" — it's a global software company with offices in America, Europe, and Asia. Security certifications are in place:

  • ISO 27001:2013 — Information security management
  • ISO 27017:2015 — Cloud security
  • ISO 27018:2019 — Personal data protection in the cloud
  • SOC 2 Type II — Data security
  • GDPR — European compliance
  • AES-256 Encryption — Data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 — Data in transit

The fundamental difference: Zoho doesn't rely on advertising — its appearance isn't just a product. Zoho makes money from selling software (CRM, ERP, Finance) — not from your data. Google wants to read your emails to improve ad targeting (even in regular Gmail — though they stopped this in Workspace). Microsoft wants to analyze you to improve its products. Zoho isn't interested in your data — and has a strict policy: "Your data is your data."

Privacy Feature: Zoho operates data centers in India and the United States. You can choose your data storage region — if your company is in Egypt or Saudi Arabia, you'll choose US. But if your company is in Europe, you can choose the Netherlands (Zoho's European data center). The option exists — unlike Google which stores only in its own centers.

Verdict: Is Zoho Right for You?

Aljehbiz Verdict

Subscribe to Zoho Mail if: Your company is small (1-20 employees). Budget is limited — you want professional email at the lowest possible cost. You don't need complex office apps. Your data privacy matters — and you hate ad tracking. Or simply: you want to start professional email today without paying a dime.
Choose Google Workspace if: Your company relies on team collaboration. You need the best email search tool in the market. Your team lives on the Google ecosystem — Analytics, Ads, YouTube. You can afford $6-12 monthly per employee.
Choose Microsoft 365 if: Your company lives on Word and Excel. You need powerful desktop apps. Teams is your team's primary communication tool. Or your company is large (+100 employees) and needs a Microsoft infrastructure.
Aljehbiz Summary: Zoho isn't the "best email service" — it's the smartest email service for companies with limited budgets. The free plan lets you start from day one at no cost. Paid plans are 6 times cheaper than Google and Microsoft with respectable competitive features. The only downsides: interface speed, office tools quality, and the "Indian company" reputation. If that doesn't matter to you — and you need clean, cheap email — Zoho is the smartest choice. But if you need the most powerful tools, pay the difference and go with Google or Microsoft.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Zoho Mail the best free Gmail alternative?

Yes — absolutely. Regular Gmail is free but with a gmail.com domain — not professional for business. Zoho's free plan gives you your own domain, no ads, with calendar and spam protection. The only difference: 5GB compared to 15GB in regular Gmail.

Is Zoho Mail secure for sensitive data?

Yes — with ISO 27001, SOC 2, and GDPR certifications. Zoho doesn't read your emails or use your data for ads. The privacy policy is very strict — stronger than regular Gmail and weaker than Google Workspace (since Workspace doesn't do ad tracking either).

I need 10 users — is Zoho suitable?

Yes — €1/month per user = €10/month for the whole team. Compared to $60/month for Google Workspace Starter and $60/month for Microsoft 365. Savings of 80%. If you want office apps with email, Workplace Standard at €4/month per user — instead of $12 for Google Business Standard and $12.50 for Microsoft Business Standard.

Does Zoho support SMTP/IMAP/POP3?

Yes — fully. You can connect Zoho Mail with any email client: Outlook, Thunderbird, Apple Mail, Samsung Mail. Instructions are available in Arabic in Zoho's help center.

Can I use Zoho for an Arabic domain?

Yes — Zoho supports any domain, including Arabic domains. The system fully supports UTF-8 — Arabic emails won't get corrupted.
