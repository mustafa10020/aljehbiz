The Beginning — From a Bedroom to a Travel Engine Serving 1.5 Billion Searches Annually

In 2004, Paul English was frustrated with the difficulty of searching for flight tickets on the internet. At a time when other sites were hiding prices and had hidden fees, he decided to build a simple tool: a search engine that compares all airlines on one screen and shows the final price with no surprises.

In 2004, he launched Kayak from a small apartment in Boston. The idea is simple: Kayak doesn't sell tickets directly — it redirects you to the airline or travel agency and takes a commission. This model kept it neutral — no need to favor one airline over another because it doesn't hold inventory.

In 2012, Booking Holdings (the parent company of Booking.com, Agoda, and Priceline) acquired it for $1.8 billion. Today, Kayak serves 1.5 billion travel searches every year, covers 100+ sites worldwide, and supports 30+ languages (including Arabic).

The name Kayak isn't about boats — it's a backronym: "Kick Ass Yet Another Kayak." The founders liked the name because it suggests speed and movement (like kayaks) — and also because it's funny.

Kayak Features Most People Don't Know

Kayak isn't just a regular flight search engine. It has advanced tools:

  • Explore: If you don't know where to go — choose "Explore" and set your budget. Kayak shows you all destinations you can reach on that amount on an interactive map (best tool for spontaneous trips)
  • Price Forecast: AI analyzes price data and tells you: "Is the ticket cheaper tomorrow or next week?" It can predict price increase or decrease with 75% accuracy
  • Price Alerts: Pick a trip and set an alert — get an email or push notification when the price drops
  • Hacker Fares: Book an outbound flight from one airline and return from another — to save money. Instead of paying $600 on one airline, you might fly Airline X outbound and Airline Y return for $400
  • Trips: Free trip organizer — collect all your booking confirmations in one place: tickets, hotels, cars (over 6,000 airports and 120,000 accommodations)
  • Multi-City: The cheapest way to travel to multiple cities — instead of booking 3 separate reservations, Kayak shows the best price combination

Kayak Hidden Hacks — How to Save 30-40%

5 hacks used by professional travelers:

  • 1. Incognito Mode: Travel sites (including Kayak) remember your search and raise the price if you return. Use private browsing (Incognito) or clear cookies before each search. Savings: 10-20%
  • 2. Day of week hacks: Kayak Price Forecast gives predictions. The obvious says "Tuesday is cheapest" — but Kayak AI analyzes specific seasons and airports. Some destinations are cheapest on Sunday, some on Thursday. Trust the forecast, not intuition
  • 3. Nearby airports: Instead of specifying "Airport X," use "Airports Nearby." Kayak searches alternative airports within 300 km. Sometimes flying from a nearby airport saves 40%
  • 4. Long layovers: Kayak shows flights with 2-8 hour stops — sometimes you can visit a new city en route. Filter "Long Layover" and choose a 5-hour stop in Dubai — see Dubai and save on the ticket
  • 5. Package deals: Kayak has "Kayak Package" — book flight + hotel + car together and save an additional 20%

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Price Forecast — AI predicts price drops with 75% accuracy
  • Explore — Interactive map by budget (better than Google Flights)
  • Hacker Fares — Book outbound and return with two different airlines
  • Price Alerts — Instant alerts on price drops
  • Kayak Trips — Free trip organizer with booking tracking
  • Simple and fast interface — easier than Skyscanner for filtering
  • Package deals — Flight + Hotel + Car = 20% savings

Cons

  • Doesn't book directly — redirects to another site (extra step)
  • Prices aren't always final — the price on Kayak may differ from the booking site by 5-10%
  • Too many ads — the web version has more ads than Google Flights
  • Doesn't verify hidden fees — some travel agencies on Kayak add fees after clicking
  • Parent company Booking Holdings — may favor its own sites (Booking, Agoda) in hotel results
