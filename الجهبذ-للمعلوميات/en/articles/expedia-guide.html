The Story of Expedia: From Microsoft's Garage to the Empire of Packages

Expedia's story is unique — it might be the only global booking platform that started inside Microsoft. In 1996, a team inside Microsoft — called "Microsoft Travel Technologies" — launched Expedia as a "side project" to explore online travel potential. Microsoft didn't know it at the time — but they had planted the seed of a giant that would later overshadow them in its field. The phrase "Bill Gates vs. Travel" has a nice ring to it — but Priceline would later buy Booking.com and change the game.

1999 was the turning point. Microsoft decided to exit the travel business — and spun off Expedia as an independent company through an IPO (Initial Public Offering). $14 per share at launch — jumped to $35 on the first day — a market value of $3 billion at the time. Thus, the independent Expedia was born.

Since then, Expedia has acquired heavily — Booking.com is different (owned by Priceline) — but Expedia focused on "the full package." The idea: instead of booking a hotel and flight separately — search once, get a combined price, save money, and book everything in one click. This is Expedia's secret weapon.

Turning Point: In 2001, Expedia acquired its biggest competitor at the time, Hotels.com. Today, Expedia Group owns more than 20 travel brands: Hotels.com, Vrbo, Orbitz, Travelocity, Hotwire, CheapTickets. A true empire of travel.

The Expedia Group Ecosystem — What Does It Own?

Expedia Group is much more than Expedia.com — it owns a network of powerful platforms:

  • Expedia.com: The main platform — full travel packages.
  • Hotels.com: Hotel-only platform — famous for "Hotels.com Rewards" (collect 10 nights, get one free).
  • Vrbo: Vacation rental platform — Airbnb's direct competitor. Strong in the US market.
  • Orbitz + Travelocity: American booking sites popular in the US market.
  • Hotwire: The "mystery deals" platform — book a hotel without knowing its name until payment — discounts of 40-60%.
  • CarRentals.com: Car rental platform — covers everything.

The power of Expedia Group lies in data — the group collects travel behavior data from more than 20 different brands.

Travel Packages — Expedia's Killer Feature

The biggest difference between Expedia and Booking.com: Booking.com is just a hotel — no flights. Expedia offers the complete trip: Hotel + Flight + Car + Activity — all in one reservation with one price. When you bundle, you save 15-30% on total cost.

How does Expedia offer lower prices than booking separately? Secret contracts — Expedia buys hotel rooms and flight seats in bulk (Wholesale) at discounted rates. When you bundle, the system automatically applies the discount. The more items you bundle, the bigger the discount. Try it yourself.

Expedia Rewards & VIP Access — The Smartest Loyalty Program

Expedia's loyalty program (Expedia Rewards) differs from Genius on Booking.com. Instead of direct discounts, Expedia gives you points (Points) on every booking — which you can use later to reduce future bookings. There are also tiers (Silver, Gold, Platinum) with additional benefits:

The VIP Access feature: selected hotels offer special discounts exclusively for Expedia Rewards members — sometimes 10-20% below the public price. Comparison between Genius and Expedia Rewards: Genius gives you direct discounts at the moment of booking. Expedia Rewards gives you long-term benefits. Which is better? Depends on your travel style — if you travel a lot for work, Genius may be better. If you take one big annual vacation, Expedia Rewards may be more suitable.

7 Pro Expedia Tips

  1. Always bundle: Even if you don't need a car — add it to the search. Sometimes adding a car makes the flight + hotel cheaper.
  2. Register for Expedia Rewards: Free — and you get points. Even one booking earns you something.
  3. Don't use only Expedia: Search on Skyscanner for flights + Booking.com for hotels — then open Expedia with the same search. Compare.
  4. Look for VIP Access: If a hotel has VIP Access status — you get 10-20% off its public price (saves a lot).
  5. Search for coupon codes: Before final payment, search "Expedia discount code" — there are often active codes (especially in the US market).
  6. Book on the app: The Expedia app often offers exclusive mobile discounts of 5-10% on some bookings.
  7. Use Hotwire for extreme deals: "Mystery deals" are worth it — book 4-star at 3-star prices.

Expedia Pros

  • Package deals — save 15-30% on combined bookings
  • Expedia Rewards — points you can use on future bookings
  • VIP Access — exclusive discounts on selected hotels
  • Full ecosystem — flights, hotels, cars, activities all in one place
  • Expedia Group — 20+ brands covering the entire travel spectrum
  • Strong support for Middle East and North African travelers

Expedia Cons

  • Flight search is weaker than dedicated search engines — Skyscanner is better
  • Hotel selection is smaller than Booking.com
  • Cancellation policy varies — some bookings are non-refundable
  • Customer support slower than Booking.com
  • Not all hotels offer VIP Access discounts
  • Best for packages — less useful for hotel-only or flight-only bookings

Verdict: When Should You Use Expedia?

Package Deals: Expedia is the undisputed king — Booking.com and Skyscanner can't compete here
Best Region: North America and long-haul travel — Expedia is the strongest
Best Use Case: If you want a full package — Expedia is your site. If you only need a hotel — Booking.com is better. If you only need a flight — Skyscanner is the best.
Weakness: Limited hotel selection compared to Booking.com — and package discounts sometimes come with restrictions
