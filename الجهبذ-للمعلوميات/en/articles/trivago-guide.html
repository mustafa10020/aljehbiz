The Beginning — From a German Idea to a Global Platform

In 2005 in Dusseldorf, Germany — Rolf Schroemgens with Stephan Stubner and Peter Vinnemeier founded Trivago. The idea: not a booking site — a price comparison site. Trivago doesn't sell hotels — it compares prices from all booking sites (Booking.com, Expedia, Hotels.com, Agoda) and shows you the cheapest.

In 2013 — 50 million monthly visits. In 2014 — acquisition by Expedia Group for $5.2 billion (the same group that owns Expedia, Hotels.com, and Vrbo). The question: did the Expedia acquisition affect Trivago's neutrality?

The answer: in some markets, yes. Trivago favors booking sites that pay higher commissions — not necessarily the cheapest for the user. But it's still a useful tool if you know how to use it smartly.

The name Trivago is derived from "Tri" (three) + "Vago" (journey in Italian) — referencing the 3 founders. And remember: Trivago doesn't book — it redirects you to the booking site. The final booking happens on Booking, Expedia, or another site.

How Trivago Works

The difference between Trivago and Booking.com is simple: Booking is a store — Trivago is a search engine. Like Skyscanner for flights. You enter the hotel name or city — Trivago searches 400+ booking sites and shows all prices on one screen.

The problem: Trivago doesn't show all prices equally — it ranks results by commission, not by price. The booking site that pays the highest commission to Trivago appears first — even if it's not the cheapest. This was the subject of complaints in the UK, Australia, and EU.

In 2026, Trivago updated the system: it now shows "room price" sorted ascending (cheapest first) but in a separate space from paid ads. You still need to focus to distinguish between ads and the real price.

Features That Set It Apart

  • Rate Indicator: Color rating system — green (excellent price), yellow (average), red (expensive). It calculates the average hotel price in the city and compares the current price against it. Best feature in Trivago — gives you an instant idea: is the price in front of you good or not?
  • Compare 400+ sites: Trivago covers famous sites (Booking, Expedia, Hotels, Agoda) and local sites in every country. In the Middle East: Almosafer, Rehlat, Wego are all present
  • Real-time price updates: Prices change in real-time — if a price drops on a booking site, Trivago shows it instantly
  • Interactive map: Display all hotels on a map with prices — see the hotel location and cost quickly
  • Advanced filtering: Filter by distance from city center, rating, star rating, and breakfast type
  • Mobile app: 4.6 stars on App Store and 4.5 on Google Play. Full Arabic support

Money-Saving Hacks with Trivago

  • 1. Ignore the first two results: Often ads. Scroll to the third or fourth result — usually the real price
  • 2. Use Rate Indicator: If the hotel shows green — the price is below the area average. A real deal
  • 3. Compare with Booking directly: After Trivago shows the cheapest price — open Booking.com yourself and compare. Sometimes Booking gives a Genius discount (10-20%) that doesn't show on Trivago
  • 4. Change currency: Sometimes booking in the local currency is cheaper than your currency. Trivago shows all currencies — try booking in local currency
  • 5. Private browsing: Like all travel sites — use Incognito Mode. Booking sites remember your search and raise the price if you return

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Compare 400+ booking sites in one place — saves huge time and effort
  • Rate Indicator — unique tool that tells you instantly if the price is good
  • Real-time price updates — see changes immediately
  • Interactive map with prices — see hotel and price on the map
  • For any hotel type — from budget to 5-star

Cons

  • Not neutral — result ranking depends on commission, not price
  • Doesn't book directly — redirects to another site (extra step)
  • Ads appear as results — you need to pay attention
  • Some booking sites aren't included in the comparison (like Airbnb)
  • Small hotel prices aren't always consistent — may not appear
  • No rewards program or loyalty points
